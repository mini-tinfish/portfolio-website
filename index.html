About

I’m an Information Technology student passionate about building secure, user-centered systems that blend creativity with strong technical foundations. I love working at the intersection of design, functionality, and cybersecurity — turning ideas into projects that are both impactful and reliable.

Currently, I’m pursuing a B.S. in Information Technology with a minor in Business Administration at Augusta University. I serve as Vice President of Girls Who Code, where I help lead technical workshops, organize collaborative cybersecurity projects, and create opportunities for women in tech to grow and connect. I’m also involved in Women in CyberSecurity and the ACM Cybersecurity Branch, expanding my knowledge in areas like governance, risk management, and defensive security.

I’ve gained experience leading teams, developing websites, and managing community initiatives — all of which strengthen my ability to connect technology with people and purpose. Whether I’m working on a new project or collaborating with others, I’m driven by curiosity and a genuine love for problem-solving.

Outside of tech, I’m a bookworm who loves getting lost in good stories, playing my flute and working on solo pieces, or passing the time fishing and catching bugs in Animal Crossing.
